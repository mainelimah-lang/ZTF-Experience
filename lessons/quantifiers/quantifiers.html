Quantifiers are used with different types of nouns to express quantities.
ZeroOneUnknown quantitySmall quantityLarge quantity
Countable Nounsno, anya/ansomefew / a fewmany, a lot of, lots of
Uncountable Nounsno, anysomelittle / a littlemuch, a lot of, lots of

  • 👉 Zero quantity

    Singular Countable Nouns

    Negative sentence + a / an + singular countable noun

    • I don’t have a dog ❌🐶
    • I don’t have an umbrella ❌☂️
    • I don’t have an excuse ❌🙄
    • I don’t have a plan ❌🧠

    Plural Countable Nouns


    Negative sentence + any + plural countable noun

    • I don’t have any questions ❌❓
    • I don’t have any meetings today ❌🗓️
    • I don’t have any messages ❌📩
    • I don’t have any options left ❌🤷

    Afirmative sentence + no + plural countable noun

    • I have no questions ❌❓
    • I have no meetings today ❌🗓️
    • I have no messages ❌📩
    • I have no options left ❌🤷

    Uncountable Nouns

    Negative sentence + any + uncountable noun

    • I don’t have any money ❌💰
    • I don’t have any time right now ❌⏰
    • I don’t have any experience in that field ❌💼
    • I don’t have any milk for the coffee ❌🥛

    Affirmative verb + no + uncountable noun

    • I have no money ❌💰
    • I have no time right now ❌⏰
    • I have no experience in that field ❌💼
    • I have no milk for the coffee ❌🥛
  • 👉 One

    Singular Countable Nouns

    Affirmative sentence + a + singular countable noun starting with consonant sound

    • I record a podcast. 🎙️
    • She found a solution quickly. ✅
    • They offered a ride home. 🚗
    • He built a website. 💻

    Affirmative sentence + an + singular countable noun starting with vowel sound

    • She received an invitation. 🎉
    • He submitted an application. 📄
    • We need an update. 🔄
    • I had an idea. 💡

  • 👉 Unknown Quantity

    Both Countable and Uncountable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + some + countable / uncountable noun

    • I invited some colleagues to the meeting. 👥
    • I need some information about the course. ℹ️
    • We bought some chairs for the office. 💺
    • They invested some money in the project. 💵

  • 👉 Small Quantity

    Plural Countable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + a few / few + plural countable noun

    • I have a few friends in this city. 👥
    • I have few friends that I can really trust. 💔
    • We plan a few activities for the weekend. 🗓️
    • I have few opportunities to practice. 🎤

    Uncountable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + a little / little + uncountable noun

    • He has a little money saved. 💰
    • I have little time to relax. ⏳
    • She shows little interest in the topic. 🥲
    • They show a little interest in the project. 👀
  • 👉 Large Quantity

    Plural Countable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + many + plural countable noun

    • I know many people in this industry. 👥
    • She handles many tasks at once. 📋
    • They face many challenges in their field. 🧠
    • I read many articles on the subject. 📰

    Uncountable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + much + uncountable noun

    • We need much information to make a decision. ℹ️
    • The company provides much support to employees. 🤝
    • I don't receive much feedback, but when I do, it's helpful. 🗣️
    • She offers much insight during discussions. 💡

    Both Countable and Uncountable Nouns

    Afirmative sentence + lots of / a lot of + countable / uncountable noun

    • She attends a lot of meetings every week. 🧑‍💼
    • We have lots of clients in this region. 💼
    • I get a lot of ideas from podcasts. 🎧
    • She gained lots of experience abroad. 🌍

    1. One
      • Affirmative sentence + a + singular countable noun starting with consonant sound
      • Affirmative sentence + an + singular countable noun starting with vowel sound

    1. Unknown Quantity
      • Afirmative sentence + some + countable / uncountable noun

    1. Small Quantity
      • Afirmative sentence + a few / few + plural countable noun
      • Afirmative sentence + a little / little + uncountable noun

    1. Large Quantity
      • Afirmative sentence + many + plural countable noun
      • Afirmative sentence + much + uncountable noun

    1. Both Countable and Uncountable Nouns
