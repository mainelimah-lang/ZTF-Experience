  • 🌍 The International Phonetic Alphabet

    💡 Imagine if every time you saw the letter “a” in English, it made the same sound. That would make life easier, right? But English doesn’t work that way. Think about these examples:

    🐱 cat
    👨‍👧 father
    ℹ️     about
    🍰 cake

    All of them have the letter “a”, but each one sounds completely different! 🤯

    English spelling is notoriously unreliable. One letter can represent many different sounds. That’s where the IPA comes in.

    • 🌍 What Is the The IPA?

      The IPA, International Phonetic Alphabet, is a writing system that uses symbols to represent the sounds of human speech, showing exactly how words are pronounced. Each symbol corresponds to the same sound across languages, helping you pronounce words accurately and confidently — a big advantage since English spelling often doesn’t match pronunciation. It helps you avoid confusion, strengthen your speaking and listening skills, and build a solid foundation from the start.

      Plus, you can apply what you learn to any other language you study. 😉

    • 💡 Meet the symbols

      Short Vowels — ɪ e æ ʌ ʊ ɒ

      Long Vowels — iː ɑː ɔː ɜː uː

      The Schwa — ə

      Diphthongs — eɪ aɪ ɔɪ aʊ oʊ ɪə eə ʊə

      Plosives — p b t d k g

      Fricatives — f v θ ð s z ʃ ʒ h

      Nasals — m n ŋ

      Liquids — l r

      Glides — w j

      Affricates — tʃ dʒ

    • ✍️ Reading IPA Transcription

      Let’s take the word transportation as an example:

      /ˌtræn . spɔːˈteɪ . ʃən/

  • The Schwa Sound 👑

    💡 The schwa sound, represented by the upside-down “e” /ə/, is produced with the tongue relaxed and positioned in the center of the mouth. This sound appears only in unstressed syllables.

    Any vowel letter (a, e, i, o, u, y) can be reduced to /ə/ sound

    • 📅 Appointment - /əˈpɔɪnt.mənt/
    • ⭐ Ability - /əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/
    • 🍋 Lemon - /ˈlem.ən/
    • 🎨 Color - /ˈkʌl.ər/
    • 👮 Police - /pəˈliːs/
    • ⏭️ Future - /ˈfjuːtʃər/
    • 🫂 Community - /kəˈmjuː.nə.ti/

    • 💉 Syringe - /səˈrɪndʒ/
