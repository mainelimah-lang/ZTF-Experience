Imperative sentences are used to issue commands, make requests, or provide instructions.

1. **Affirmative and Negative Forms**: Imperative sentences can be affirmative (e.g., "Do your homework") or negative (e.g., "Do not touch that").

**Types of Imperative Sentences**

- **Positive Imperatives**: These give commands or requests that encourage action (e.g., "Finish your meal").
- **Negative Imperatives**: These instruct someone not to do something (e.g., "Don't run in the hall").
- **Exclamatory Imperatives**: These convey urgency or strong emotion and may end with an exclamation mark (e.g., "Watch out!")

1. **Politeness**: Adding words like "please" can soften the command, making it a polite request (e.g., "Please pass the salt")
