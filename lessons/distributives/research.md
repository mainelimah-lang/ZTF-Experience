Distributives help provide clarity and specificity when discussing the distribution, inclusion, exclusion, or choice within a set or group.

Distributives are words used to discuss the parts of a group in relation to the whole group.

- *all*
- *any*
- *both*
- *each*
- *either*
- *every*
- *half*
- *neither*
- *none*

**Each:**

1. Each student has a textbook.
2. Each person should bring their own lunch.
3. Each day brings new opportunities.
4. Each member of the team has a role to play.
5. Each child received a toy at the party.

**Every:**

1. Every student must attend class.
2. Every morning, I have a cup of coffee.
3. Every house has a front door.
4. Every day is a chance to learn something new.
5. Every book on the shelf is worth reading.

**Either:**

1. You can choose either the red or the blue pen.
2. Either option is fine with me.
3. Either you come with us or stay here.
4. You can either call or send an email.
5. Either way, we'll get there on time.

**Neither:**

1. Neither book is mine; they belong to my friend.
2. Neither option suits my needs.
3. Neither of the students completed the assignment.
4. I have neither time nor money to go on vacation.
5. Neither candidate was selected for the position.

**Both:**

1. Both children are playing in the park.
2. Both options are equally good.
3. Both of my parents work full-time.
4. I have friends from both Germany and France.
5. Both books are interesting to read.
