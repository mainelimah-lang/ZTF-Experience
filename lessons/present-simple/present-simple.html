💡 Present Simple

Have you ever noticed how some things in your life tend not to change? 🌍

Maybe you always wake up at the same time ⏰, eat the same breakfast 🍞, and think the same thoughts while brushing your teeth 🦷.

Maybe you take the same way to work 🛣️, stop at the same traffic lights🚦, and complain about traffic — because it never changes 😤.

At work, it’s the same story — you check emails 📧, attend meetings 👥, solve problems 🧠, and then do it all over again the next day 🔁.

And outside, ☀️ The sun shines. 🌬️ The wind blows. 🌧️ It rains. — just as it always does.

These small patterns, habits, and facts are part of our daily lives — and that’s exactly what the Present Simple helps us describe:

✅ Facts
Always true (universal or scientific facts).

• The sun rises in the east.

• A year has 12 months.

• Water boils at 100 °C.

• Lightning comes before thunder.

👍 General Truths
Usually true (typical behaviors)

• Parents worry about their children.

• Children love cartoons.

• Smartphones distract people easily.

• Exercise improves your mood.

🔁 Routines and Habits
Repeated actions

• I wake up at 7 a.m. every day.

• We have lunch at 12:30.

• He walks his dog after dinner.

• I take a shower before bed.

🪴 Long-Term Situations
True now and for some time

• I live in a small apartment downtown.

• She works for a tech company.

• They have two kids.

• We own a small bakery.

👉 Patterns

We have talked about when we use the Present Simple. Now let’s explore how we use it:

✅ Affirmative Sentences

▪️ 1st & 2nd Person Singular & Plural [I, You, We, They]

▪️ 3rd Person Singular [He, She ,It]

  • If the verb ends in:
    • s, -sh, -ch, -o, -x, -z → Add "es"
    • Consonant + y → Remove "y" and add "ies"
    • Other verbs → Add "s"
    • Exception: have → has
❌ Negative Sentences

▪️ 1st & 2nd Person Singular & Plural [I, You, We, They]

💡 don’t = do + not

▪️3rd Person Singular [He, She ,It]

💡 doesn’t = does + not

❓ Yes/No Questions

▪️ 1st & 2nd Person Singular & Plural [I, You, We, They]

👍 Yes, Subject + do.

👎 No, Subject + don’t.

▪️ 3rd Person Singular [He, She ,It]

👍 Yes, Subject + does.

👎 No, Subject + doesn’t.

❓ Wh-Questions

▪️ 1st & 2nd Person Singular & Plural [I, You, We, They]

▪️ 3rd Person Singular [He, She ,It]

🧰🔧 Your Grammar Toolbox

Base verb = verb without “to”

Auxiliarly verbs Simple Present tense:
  • 👍 Do
  • 👎 Don’t = do + not

  • 👍 Does
  • 👎 Doesn’t = does + not

🗣️ Pronunciation
  • Do - /du:/
  • Does - /dʌz/
  • Don’t - /doʊnt/
  • Doesn’t - /ˈdʌz.ənt/
