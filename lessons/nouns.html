💡 Nouns are words that name people, places, things, or ideas. They're like the labels that help us identify and talk about everything in our world. Without “Nouns” we couldn't talk about anything specific - we'd just be pointing and saying "that thing over there".

Think of nouns as the "name tags" of the English language.

  • 👥 Collective Nouns
    A group of people, animals, or things.
    • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 team – a group of people working together
    • 👨‍🏫 staff – a group of employees
    • 👨‍🎓 class – a group of students
    • 👨‍👨‍👧‍👦 family – people related by blood or marriage
    • 💼 committee – a group organized for a specific task
    • 🧑‍💻 panel – experts discussing a topic
    • 👮 crew – people who work together on a ship or plane / airplane
    • 🧠 audience – people watching a performance or event
    • 🎤 band – musicians playing together
    • 🧑‍🔬 group – a general word for any collection
    • 🐑 flock – a group of sheep or birds
    • 🐘 herd – a group of large animals like elephants or cows
    • 🐟 school – a group of fish
    • 🐝 swarm – a group of insects, especially bees
    • 🦁 pride – a group of lions
    • 🐺 pack – a group of wolves or dogs
    • 🐜 colony – a group of ants or bacteria
    • 📚 library – a collection of books
    • 📁 archive – a collection of historical documents
    • 🎨 collection – a set of objects (art, clothes, stamps)
    • 🛍️ range – a group of related products
    • 🏘️ neighborhood – a group of houses or people in an area
    • 🎓 faculty – a group of teachers at a school or college
    • 📊 survey – a group of collected responses
    • 🧑‍⚖️ jury – a group of people deciding in court
    • 🧑‍🎤 cast – a group of actors in a play or film
