## Teaching

## 9.1 Teaching & Learning Tips

**Effective Online Teaching Methods** [6](https://games4esl.com/teaching-wiki/how-to-teach-the-past-simple-tense/):

**Context-First Approach**: Always start with a timeline visual showing past, present, and future to establish the concept [6](https://games4esl.com/teaching-wiki/how-to-teach-the-past-simple-tense/). Use familiar situations from students' lives to introduce Past Simple naturally [12](https://busyteacher.org/13649-teaching-simple-past-7-simple-activities.html).

**Encourage Personal Sharing**: After teaching the structure, prompt students with questions like:

- "What did you do last weekend?"
- "Tell us about your best childhood memory"
- "Describe your morning routine yesterday"

**Interactive Online Games** [17](https://www.gamestolearnenglish.com/past-tense-game/):

- **Past Tense Charades**: Students act out actions while others describe what they did using Past Simple [12](https://busyteacher.org/13649-teaching-simple-past-7-simple-activities.html)
- **Digital Storytelling**: Students create collaborative stories where each person adds one Past Simple sentence [18](https://teambuilding.com/blog/online-classroom-games)
- **Virtual Time Travel**: Students "visit" different time periods and describe what they saw and did [18](https://teambuilding.com/blog/online-classroom-games)

**Quick Comprehension Checks**:

- "Tell me one thing you did before this class"
- "What's the past tense of 'eat'?"
- "Make this sentence negative: 'I went to the store'"
- "Ask me a question using Past Simple"

**Infographic Templates**: Use platforms like Piktochart or Canva to create Past Simple infographics showing:

- Regular vs. irregular verb formations

**Video Resources**: Utilize educational YouTube channels that demonstrate Past Simple through visual storytelling and animated examples 21.

**Interactive Whiteboards**: Use tools like Jamboard or Miro for collaborative verb conjugation exercises where students can drag and drop correct forms [22](https://avidopenaccess.org/resource/wordwall/).
