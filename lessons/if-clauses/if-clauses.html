If-Clauses

Cover

Number24

💡 An if-clause or conditional sentence is a sentence structure that connects a cause and an effect — when one thing happens, another always happens as a result.

In short

“If this happens, then that happens.”

  • General Structure
  • Types of Conditionals

    • 👉 Zero Conditional - If + present simple, present simple

      ✅ General truths, scientific facts, and situations that always happen when the condition is met.

      Examples:

      • If you heat water to 100°C, it boils. 🔥💧
      • If it rains, the ground gets wet. 🌧️💦
      • If you don’t water the plants, they die. 💧🚫🪴
      • If you mix red and blue, you get purple. 🔴🔵🟣

    • 👉 First Conditional - If + present simple, future simple

      🔮 Real and possible situations in the future — things that are likely to happen if the condition is true.

      Examples:

      • If you touch that wire, you’ll get a shock. ⚡🧤
      • If we leave now, we’ll catch the bus. 🚌⏰
      • If the temperature drops, it will snow. 🌡️❄️
      • If the traffic is bad, we’ll be late. 🚦⏳

    • 👉 Second Conditional - If + past simple, would + base verb

      💭 Unreal or imaginary situations in the present or future — things that probably won’t happen, but we imagine them.

      Examples:

      • If everyone spoke the same language, communication would be easier. 🌐🗣️
      • If I were a millionaire, I would travel the world. 💰✈️
      • If we lived by the beach, we would go surfing every day. 🏄‍♂️
      • If people didn’t use plastic, the planet would be cleaner. 🌱

      • 💡➕ The Subjunctive Mood

        The subjunctive mood is used to talk about unreal, hypothetical, or imaginary situations — things that aren’t true, but we imagine them.

        That’s why, in the Second Conditional, we use “were” for all subjects (I, he, she, it, you, we, they), instead of "was" for (I, He, She, It).

        SubjectExample
        IIf I were rich, I would buy a house by the ocean. 🌊🏡
        YouIf you were more organized, life would be easier. 🗂️🙂
        HeIf he were taller, he could play basketball professionally. 🏀⛹️‍♂️
        SheIf she were the boss, she would change the rules. 💼📋
        ItIf it were warmer today, we would go for a walk. 🌤️🚶‍♂️
        WeIf we were in New York now, we’d visit Times Square. 🗽🗺️
        TheyIf they were more careful, they wouldn’t make so many mistakes. ⚠️📝

    • 👉 Third Conditional - If + past perfect, would/could/might have + past participle

      😔 Unreal situations in the past — regrets or things that could have happened differently but didn’t.

      • Would have → certain result …💡 I’m sure about the result (in imagination)

        If he had joined us, he would have enjoyed the trip ✈️

        If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam. 🎓

        If the weather had been better, we would have gone to the beach. 🌴

      • Could have → possibility / ability …💡 It was possible, but not certain.

        If he had joined us, he could have enjoyed the trip ✈️

        If I had studied harder, I could have passed the exam. 🎓

        If the weather had been better, we could have gone to the beach. 🌴

      • Might have → maybe / uncertainty … 💡 Maybe yes, maybe no

        If he had joined us, he might have enjoyed the trip ✈️

        If I had studied harder, I might have passed the exam. 🎓

        If the weather had been better, we might have gone to the beach. 🌴
