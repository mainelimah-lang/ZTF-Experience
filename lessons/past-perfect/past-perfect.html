💡 The Past Perfect tense is used when you want to talk about something that happened before another thing in the past.

It's like saying "this happened first, then that happened" but both things are already finished.

  • 👉 Pattern

    • ✅ Affirmative Sentences
    • ❌ Negative Sentences

    • ❓ Yes/No Questions

      👍 Yes, Subject + had.

      👎 No, Subject + hadn’t.

    • ❓ Wh-Questions

  • 🧰🔧 Your Grammar Toolbox

    📃Verb List

    • Auxiliarly verbs Past Perfect tense:
    • 👍 Had

      🗣️ /hæd/
    • 👎 Hadn’t = had + not

      🗣️ /ˈhæd.ənt/

  • ⌛ Time Expressions

    The Past Perfect often uses time expressions to connect two events and show the timeline between them.

    • Before - They had had many problems before they found a good solution. 🛠️
    • After - After I had sent the message, I realized I had texted the wrong person. 📱😅
    • By the time - By the time we reached the beach, the sun had already set. 🌅
    • When - When I got to the station, the train had left. 🚆
    • Already - We had already had lunch before the guests arrived. 🍽️
    • Just - I had just sat down to relax when the doorbell rang. 🔔
    • Never - I had never traveled abroad before 2015. ✈️
    • Ever - It was the best movie I had ever seen. 📽️
    • Until - I hadn’t realized how much I missed home until I had been away for a year. 🏡
    • Once - Once I had tasted the dessert, I couldn’t stop eating it. 🍮
