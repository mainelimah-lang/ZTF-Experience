Numbers play a vital role in our daily communication, and mastering them in English can be challenging for Brazilian students due to interference from Portuguese. Here are some common issues to be aware of:

- **Pronunciation Differences**: English numbers may sound similar to Portuguese, but subtle differences can lead to misunderstandings.

- **Number Order and Structure**: The way numbers are organized in English can differ significantly from Portuguese, especially with large numbers.

- **Decimal Points and Commas**: In English, decimal points and commas are used differently than in Portuguese, which can lead to confusion.

- **Plural Forms**: Students often mistakenly pluralize certain numeric expressions, which is incorrect in English.

- **Ordinal vs. Cardinal Numbers**: Understanding when to use ordinal (first, second) versus cardinal (one, two) numbers is essential.

Being aware of these common mistakes will help you focus on these areas and improve your numerical fluency in English.
