# Demonstrative Pronouns: A Comprehensive Guide for Beginner English Learners

## 1 ‚Äì Analogy: The Magic Pointing Wands

~~Think of demonstrative pronouns as **magic pointing wands** that help you point to specific things without having to name them~~ over and over again[1](https://www.scribbr.com/nouns-and-pronouns/demonstrative-pronouns/). Just like a magician uses a wand to point at different objects during a show, we use demonstrative pronouns (this, that, these, those) to point at people, places, or things around us[2](https://myenglishgrammar.com/lessons/this-that-these-and-those/)[3](https://www.thesaurus.com/e/grammar/demonstrative-pronouns/).

Imagine you're standing in a room with objects both near and far from you. Your **magic pointing wand** has four different "spells":

- **"This"** - points to ONE thing close to you (like pointing to a book in your hand)
- **"That"** - points to ONE thing far from you (like pointing to a clock on the wall across the room)
- **"These"** - points to MANY things close to you (like pointing to pencils on your desk)
- **"Those"** - points to MANY things far from you (like pointing to cars in a parking lot)

**Definition**: ~~Demonstrative pronouns are special words that replace nouns and help us point to specific people, places, or things~~[1](https://www.scribbr.com/nouns-and-pronouns/demonstrative-pronouns/)[2](https://myenglishgrammar.com/lessons/this-that-these-and-those/). They show whether something is near or far from the speaker, and whether we're talking about one thing (singular) or many things (plural)[4](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/umes-englishcomp1/chapter/demonstrative-pronouns/)[5](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/parts-of-speech/demonstrative-pronouns/). We use them when both the speaker and listener know what we're referring to, making our communication faster and clearer[5](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/parts-of-speech/demonstrative-pronouns/).

## 2 ‚Äì Awareness: Why Demonstrative Pronouns Matter

Learning demonstrative pronouns is **crucial** for English learners because they appear in everyday conversations constantly[6](https://www.off2class.com/lesson-plan-downloads/teaching-demonstrative-pronouns-the-easy-way/)[7](https://www.yourdictionary.com/articles/this-that-these-those-guide). These words help you:

- **Avoid repetition**: Instead of saying "Can you pass me the book, the red book on the table," you can simply say "Can you pass me that?"[5](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/parts-of-speech/demonstrative-pronouns/)
- **Make introductions**: "This is my friend Sarah" or "These are my classmates"[7](https://www.yourdictionary.com/articles/this-that-these-those-guide)
- **Ask for clarification**: "What is this?" or "ƒ∑Whose are those?"[8](https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/demonstratives)
- **Give directions**: "Put that over there" or "Bring these here"[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)
- **Sound more natural**: Native speakers use demonstrative pronouns frequently in phone conversations, shopping, and daily interactions[7](https://www.yourdictionary.com/articles/this-that-these-those-guide)[8](https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/demonstratives)

Without mastering demonstrative pronouns, your English will sound repetitive and unnatural, making communication less efficient10.

## 3 ‚Äì Struggles: Common Difficulties and Mistakes

## Beginner Level Mistakes:

1. **Singular/Plural Confusion**: Students often mix up "this/these" or "that/those"11. Example mistake: "This books are expensive" instead of "These books are expensive"
2. **Pronunciation Problems**: The "th" sound is difficult for many learners, and distinguishing between the short "i" in "this" versus the long "ee" in "these" is challenging1213[14](https://www.busuu.com/en/english/demonstrative-pronouns)
3. **Distance Confusion**: Students struggle with when to use "near" (this/these) versus "far" (that/those) demonstratives[15](https://talkpal.ai/grammar/mastering-english-demonstratives-a-comprehensive-guide/)[16](https://english.lingolia.com/en/vocabulary/confusing-words/this-that-these-those)

## Advanced Level Mistakes:

1. **Overuse**: Using demonstrative pronouns too frequently instead of varying language10
2. **Missing Referents**: Using demonstrative pronouns without clear context about what they refer to11[15](https://talkpal.ai/grammar/mastering-english-demonstratives-a-comprehensive-guide/)
3. **Time and Emotional Distance**: Not understanding that "near/far" can refer to time ("this morning" vs "that time long ago") or emotional closeness[4](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/umes-englishcomp1/chapter/demonstrative-pronouns/)[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)

## 4 ‚Äì Grammar Overview

**Demonstrative pronouns** are words that replace nouns to point to specific people, places, or things[1](https://www.scribbr.com/nouns-and-pronouns/demonstrative-pronouns/)[3](https://www.thesaurus.com/e/grammar/demonstrative-pronouns/). English has four demonstrative pronouns:

| **Distance** | **Singular** | **Plural** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Near** | this | these |
| **Far** | that | those |

**When to use them**:

- When the noun is already known from context[5](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/parts-of-speech/demonstrative-pronouns/)
- To avoid repeating the same noun multiple times[5](https://www.grammarly.com/blog/parts-of-speech/demonstrative-pronouns/)
- To point out specific items in relation to the speaker's location[4](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/umes-englishcomp1/chapter/demonstrative-pronouns/)
- In introductions and phone conversations[7](https://www.yourdictionary.com/articles/this-that-these-those-guide)

## 5 ‚Äì Structure & Rules

## Formation Rules:

1. **Distance Rule**:
    - **This/These** = close to the speaker[2](https://myenglishgrammar.com/lessons/this-that-these-and-those/)[16](https://english.lingolia.com/en/vocabulary/confusing-words/this-that-these-those)
    - **That/Those** = far from the speaker[2](https://myenglishgrammar.com/lessons/this-that-these-and-those/)[16](https://english.lingolia.com/en/vocabulary/confusing-words/this-that-these-those)
2. **Number Rule**:
    - **This/That** = singular (one item)[1](https://www.scribbr.com/nouns-and-pronouns/demonstrative-pronouns/)[4](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/umes-englishcomp1/chapter/demonstrative-pronouns/)
    - **These/Those** = plural (multiple items)[1](https://www.scribbr.com/nouns-and-pronouns/demonstrative-pronouns/)[4](https://courses.lumenlearning.com/umes-englishcomp1/chapter/demonstrative-pronouns/)

## 7 ‚Äì Vocabulary & Phrases

## Common Collocations:

- **"This is"** + noun/name (introductions)[7](https://www.yourdictionary.com/articles/this-that-these-those-guide)
- **"That's"** + adjective ("That's great!"[8](https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/demonstratives), "That's terrible!")
- **"These are"** + plural noun[17](https://english.eagetutor.com/spoken-english-grammar/the-right-use-of-this-that-these-those)
- **"Those were"** + time expression ("Those were good times")[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)

## Useful Expressions:

- **"What's this/that?"** - asking for identification[18](https://games4esl.com/lesson-plans/this-that/)[19](https://www.eslkidstuff.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/demonstrative-pronouns-lesson-plan-US.pdf)
- **"This way/that way"** - giving directions
- **"This time/that time"** - referring to specific moments[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)
- **"This one/that one"** - choosing between options[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)
- **"These days/those days"** - talking about time periods[9](https://www.ef.com/wwen/english-resources/english-grammar/demonstratives/)

## Adverbs Often Used Together:

- **"This here"** / **"That there"** - emphasizing location[16](https://english.lingolia.com/en/vocabulary/confusing-words/this-that-these-those)
- **"These over here"** / **"Those over there"** - showing specific placement[16](https://english.lingolia.com/en/vocabulary/confusing-words/this-that-these-those)

## 8 ‚Äì Pronunciation Factors

## Stress Patterns:

- **Strong stress** on demonstrative pronouns when contrasting: "Not **THAT** one, **THIS** one!"21
- **Reduced stress** when used naturally in conversation21

## Intonation Patterns:

- **Rising intonation** for questions: "Is **THIS** ‚Üó yours?"
- **Falling intonation** for statements: "**THAT** ‚Üò is correct."
- **Emphatic stress** when showing strong emotions: "**THIS** is incredible!"21

## Common Pronunciation Issues:

1. **"Th" sound**: Many learners struggle with the initial /Œ∏/ sound12
2. **Vowel distinction**: "This" /√∞…™s/ vs "these" /√∞iÀêz/1213
3. **Final sounds**: "That" /√∞√¶t/ vs "those" /√∞o äz/13

## Tips for Better Pronunciation:

- **Practice minimal pairs**: "this/these," "that/those"12
- **Use gestures** while practicing to reinforce meaning[22](https://www.onestopenglish.com/support-for-teaching-grammar/demonstratives-tips-and-activities/144934.article)
- **Record yourself** and compare with native speakers12

    ### Introduction to Demonstrative Pronouns
    
    Hello, future English experts! Today, we‚Äôre going to explore a fun and practical part of English called demonstrative pronouns. Think of these as the words that help you "point" at things without using your finger. Imagine you're in a room full of interesting objects. Instead of saying each object's name, you can use demonstrative pronouns to refer to them easily.

    # Demonstrative Pronouns: Comprehensive Guide
    
    ## 1. Definition and Purpose
    - **Demonstrative Pronouns** are pronouns used to point to specific things or people, helping to specify which one(s) you are talking about without repeating the noun.
    
    ## 2. List of Demonstrative Pronouns
    - **This**: Refers to a singular noun that is close in distance or time.
    - **These**: Refers to plural nouns that are close in distance or time.
    - **That**: Refers to a singular noun that is farther away in distance or time.
    - **Those**: Refers to plural nouns that are farther away in distance or time.
    
    ## 3. Usage and Context
    - **Proximity**: 
      - Use "this" and "these" for things nearby.
      - Use "that" and "those" for things farther away.
      - Example: "This is my book." vs. "That is your book."
    
    - **Number**:
      - Use "this" and "that" for singular nouns.
      - Use "these" and "those" for plural nouns.
      - Example: "These are my keys." vs. "Those are your keys."
    
    ## 4. Demonstrative Pronouns vs. Demonstrative Adjectives
    - **Pronouns**: Replace the noun entirely, as in ‚ÄúThis is mine.‚Äù
    - **Adjectives**: Modify the noun, as in ‚ÄúThis book is mine.‚Äù
    
    ## 5. Contextual Examples
    - **Proximity Examples**:
      - "This is delicious." (Holding a piece of cake)
      - "That was a great movie." (Talking about a movie seen last week)
    
    - **Preference and Comparison**:
      - "I prefer this dress to that one because of its color."
    
    - **General Reference**:
      - "This is what I believe: honesty is the best policy."
      - "Those were unforgettable times we spent traveling across Europe."
    
    - **Introducing People or Ideas**:
      - "This is Sarah, my colleague from the marketing department."
      - "That is exactly what we need to improve our process."
    
    ## 6. Special Rules and Tips
    - **Abstract Ideas**: Demonstrative pronouns can refer to ideas or experiences.
      - Example: "That was an amazing experience."
    
    - **Emphasis**: Sometimes used for emphasis or to express emotions.
      - Example: "Those were the days!"
    
    ## 7. Vocabulary and Expressions to Enhance Understanding
    - **Related Vocabulary**:
      - **Near**: Used to describe objects close to the speaker.
      - **Far**: Used to describe objects further away from the speaker.
      - **Object Vocabulary**: Common nouns like "book," "car," "house," "flower," etc.
    
    - **Spatial Expressions**:
      - **Here**: Used with "this" and "these" for items close to the speaker.
      - **There**: Used with "that" and "those" for items further away.
    
    - **Time Expressions**:
      - **Now**: Often used with "this" and "these" to indicate current time or situation.
      - **Then**: Often used with "that" and "those" to refer to past events or situations.
    
    - **Comparative Phrases**:
      - "This is better than that."
      - "These are more expensive than those."
    
    - **Clarifying Questions**:
      - "Which one?"
      - "Do you mean this or that?"
      - "Are you referring to these or those?"
    
    - **Expressions to Indicate Preference**:
      - "I prefer this/that."
      - "I like these/those better."
    
    - **Descriptive Adjectives**:
      - Using adjectives to give more information, e.g., "this red book," "those tall buildings."
    
    - **Quantifiers**:
      - Phrases like "a few of these," "many of those," to combine with demonstrative pronouns for quantity expressions.
    
    - **Contextual Phrases**:
      - "Look at this!"
      - "What is that over there?"
      - "These are my favorite."
      - "Those were the days."

Practice through dialogues, role-plays, and situational exercises will further solidify their learning.
